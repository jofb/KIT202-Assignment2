Movie Blog

The Batman

22nd March 2022

A new take on the dark knight, Robert Pattinson had big shoes to fill following the Dark Knight Trilogy but made the role his own. A much darker Gotham helps to immerse the viewer into the city, with a chilling villain in Paul Danos' Riddler leaving viewers on the edge of their seat. The climax of the film was satisfying and built up a world I wanted to see more of, leaving the door open for potenital sequels. 9/10

The Matrix: Resurrections

26th December 2021

A highly unexpected return to the Matrix franchise, featuring the return of Keanu Reeves and Carrie-Anne Moss. This film reminds us why the original was an instant hit, but only through inferior rehashes of classic moments and action setpieces from The Matrix 1. Everything that made The Matrix unique and mind-blowing was made bland and formulaic in The Matrix: Resurrections, from the martial arts choerography to the visual effects, to the attempt to portray Agent Smith without Hugo Weaving. This said, fans of the deeper lore of The Matrix 2 and 3 may have a reason to watch. 3.5/10

Venom: Let There Be Carnage

25th November 2021

Tom Hardy hams it up as both Eddie Brock and his alien buddy/roommate/parasite Venom. While it is a shorter sequel to 'Venom', it manages to fit in more action sequences and comedic scenes. Carnage was portrayed about as well as you can expect from a PG-13 adaptaion of the character. Overall an enjoyable movie that doesn't push the boundaries, but doesn't fall too short either. 7/10

No Time To Die

10th October 2021

One of the most acclaimed film series of all time delivered a closing chapter to Daniel Craigs' James Bond portrayal. While it feels like a classic Bond film with the pacing and cinematography, the ending was a suprise yet felt inevitable as the movie progressed. As per, the stakes were high for Bond but the supporting characters added an extra layer in his strive to succeed against the odds. A great finale for Craig in the role and I am interested to see what the future holds for 007. 8.5/10
